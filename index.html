Meeting Cost Calculator

Turn attendee time into a dollar figure — instantly.

  1. Search for a job title in one or more attendee rows — salaries are pulled from organizational pay data.
  2. Set the quantity for each role to reflect how many people with that title are in the meeting.
  3. Enter the meeting duration in minutes.
  4. The calculator divides each annual salary by 2,080 work-hours, then multiplies by the duration and headcount to show the real labor cost of the meeting.
Notes on salary figures
  • Duplicate positions are averaged. When multiple employees share the same job title, their individual salaries are averaged into a single figure. The value shown for a title represents the mean annual salary across all people in that role.
  • Use Quantity for same-title attendees. Rather than adding a separate row for each person, set the Quantity field to the number of attendees with that title. The calculator multiplies the averaged salary by that count.
  • Salary figures are annual base pay. Values reflect base annual compensation only and do not include benefits, overtime, or other compensation components.
  • 2,080-hour work year. The hourly rate is derived by dividing annual salary by 2,080 (52 weeks × 40 hours), which is the standard full-time equivalent used for cost calculations.
